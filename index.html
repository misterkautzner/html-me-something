Beginning

John first came into contact with computer science while pursuing his bachelor's degree at Southeast Missouri State University. He took a class in C++ and loved it. He thought it was so cool that you could "solve problems once and for all" by writing a program that could solve a generalized problem. It took a higher initial investment of time than calculating something by hand, but eventually, if the problem needed to be solved enough times with different parameters, the program could save a LOT of time.



Rediscovery

After graduating, John became an Adjunct instructor at a community college. Unfortunately, John wanted a career and his position with the college was only part-time. When he saw the full list of adjunct instructors in the math department, he realized it would be years before he could become a fulltime instructor. John needed to get into a new field. After talking with a friend in the tech field, he decided to pursue programming.

John used online resources and guidance from his friend to get started. However, After a while it became very difficult to find resources at the right level.



Bootcamp

John decided it would be worth it to join a programming bootcamp because of the time it would save him. He enrolled in one. However, he did not receive the education he expected. There were major problems with the program, but he was able to learn enough to get a job in the field.



Working in the Field

As a software developer, John was behind in terms of skill. He struggled a lot, but made improvements over time. His company found a place where he could become successful.



LaunchCode

John really wanted to improve himself professionally. In order to do so, he knew that he would have to strengthen his fundamentals and learn the things he had missed along the way. He joined LaunchCode to this end. He didn't learn much new in the first Unit, but by the first week of Unit 2, he could tell that he was going to learn a lot. Stay tuned...
